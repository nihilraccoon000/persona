The BoA ATM

A sketch of a typical BoA ATM for drawing and depositing cash. Users use the number pad to insert passwords or cash amount, insert/take their cards/cash from the corresponding outlets, and use the touch screen to interact with most of the instructions. Today's BoA ATMs also come with a tap zone for contactless transaction.

My observations:
Most users are either students or staff. Most interactions with the ATM went smooth.
People processed at slightly different paces. Some got their goal completed very quickly, some would gaze at the screen for a bit longer.
The common process most people follow without looking at any instructions: Insert card -- Input password -- Interact with screen


I asked the ATM users about the following:

  1. Are you here for depositing or drawing or anything else? Have you encountered any problems?
  2. Do you find the process straightforward?
  3. (A not-so-relevant bonus question that I asked some people out of curiosity:)
    Have you noticed the tap zone? Are you using/will you be using NFC instead of a physical card? Do you find it more convenient/Do you think it'd be more convenient?

Summarizing the answers:

  • Most people use the ATM mainly to draw cash, and the process is straightforward for people.
  • The process is straightforward for people
  • (Addressing the bonus question above)
    People who are using NFC find it convenient.
    People who are not using NFC can hardly notice the tap zone, and they tend to not use it because they think using a physical card is not that inconvenient; some also express security concerns.

I constructed 2 personas:

Note: The personas are based more on my long-termed observation, rather than just the interview above.

Alex, a student who wants to buy their lunch before class, at a student-run Cafe that only accepts cash.

They're unlikely to have any issues with the interface, since their intention does not involve complex operations, plus they're familiar with the interface.

(This persona represents a typical college student that is commonly seen around on-campus ATMs -- familiar with basic ATM operations, often uses the ATM to draw cash.)

KK, a new international student who wants to deposit the cash they brought.

Unlike the other persona whose usage of the ATM is unplanned, "depositing the cash they brought" is something they'd put on their schedule.

Inserting the card, inserting password, interacting with the touch screen should not be difficult for them, though it might take them a short while to figure out how to deposit cash.

(This persona represents new users of the ATM -- they usually have a planned goal to complete (even if the goal is just to "try out the ATM"). They will not necessarily experience any hardship, but the whole process is new to them.)
